In fact, the small shapes concerned don't have to be squares. They could be some other 2D shape, perhaps not identical one to the other.
Again, as long as the division is arranged so that all the shapes have area less than \(\epsilon\) (where \(\epsilon\) goes to \(0\) as the divisions get finer) and all the shapes have diameter less than \(\delta\) (where \(\delta\) goes to \(0\) as the divisions get finer – by diameter of a shape we mean the distance of the furthest two points of the shape), the sums will tend to the same unique value.
