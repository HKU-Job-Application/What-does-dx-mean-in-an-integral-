A typical query is to integrate a real function \(f(x,y)\) over some 2-D shape on the \(xy\) plane. We could consider \(\displaystyle\int_C\exp(-x^2-y^2)\,\text{d}x\,\text{d}y\), where \(C\) is a disc centred at the origin. Let's use a radius of \(\sqrt{2}\), so that \(C\) is the set of points where \(x^2+y^2\leq 2\). What does the integral mean, mathematically?
By the way, before we explore that, do make sure you know how to use the Desmos graphing website, by going to desmos.com/3d and typing in this line:
0<z<\left\{x^{2}+y^{2}<2\ :\ \exp\left(-x^{2}-y^{2}\right)\right\}
It fills in, in solid colour, the exact volume being calculated in the integral. It looks like a flying saucer or something, with a thin circular dish at the bottom.
