Just as \(\displaystyle\int_{[a,b]}f(x)\,\text{d}x\) divides the interval into little sub‑intervals, multiplies the length of the sub‑interval by some value of the function, and then adds up the rectangular areas,
so \(\displaystyle\int_C f(x,y)\,\text{d}x\,\text{d}y\) divides \(C\) into little squares, multiplies the area of each sub‑square by some value of the function, and then adds up the volumes of the cuboids.
You can move the slider above the graph of \(C\), to change the number of squares (or equivalently, the size of each square). Clicking on each small square automatically brings up on the left what the volume is over that square, which looks like a cuboid.
\( f(x,y) = e^{-x^{2}-y^{2}} \) on \(x^2+y^2\le 2\)

📐 Riemann estimate (0.20) : 2.3710

🎯 Exact integral : \(2.7164\)
